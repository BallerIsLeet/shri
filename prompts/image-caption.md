# Image & carousel caption — style guide

Used by the carousel + image finalize step. Writes the ready-to-paste social copy that ships next to the asset.

## Anatomy of a caption

1. **Lead.** One line that earns the click into "more". Often the same hook as slide 1, or a tighter version of it.
2. **Body.** 2-5 short paragraphs that deliver the idea the asset teases. Each paragraph is one beat.
3. **CTA.** One ask. Specific. ("Reply with what you built this week." / "Link in bio.")
4. **Hashtags / mentions.** Platform-dependent (see below).

Keep total length under ~600 chars on TikTok/Reels (most viewers don't expand), up to ~1500 on Instagram feed.

## Platform conventions

| Platform | Convention |
|---|---|
| `TIKTOK` | 3-5 hashtags inline at the end. No emojis in the hook. |
| `REELS` / `IG_FEED` | 5-10 hashtags hidden after a line break of dots, or in the first comment. Emojis OK in moderation. |
| `SHORTS` | Caption is mostly ignored; keep it to one punchy line + 2 hashtags. |
| `X` | No hashtags. 280-char hard limit. Lead with the hook; cut everything else. |

## TO PERSONALIZE

- The brand's tone register: snarky vs warm vs neutral.
- Any phrases / words to use repeatedly (signature language).
- Phrases / words to avoid (corporate buzzwords, competitor names, etc.).
- Default CTA style — "DM us X", "reply with Y", "link in bio", or no CTA when the asset speaks for itself.

## Always include

- A caption that could ship without edits.
- A single CTA, never stacked.
- Platform-appropriate hashtag treatment.

## What never to do

- Never start a caption with "In today's post we'll..." or any tutorial framing.
- Never use the word "engaging" or "amazing".
- Never write a CTA that asks for more than one action.
- Never duplicate the hook verbatim from the asset to the caption — vary the phrasing.
